Why Do We Feel Anxious?

When you're experiencing anxiousness, your thoughts are being disrupted by the emotional parts of your brain which is comporomised of your brain's limbic system.

Common Anxiety Disorders

Generalized Anxiety

The excessive worry and fear that interferes with a persons everyday life. People with this disorder become anxious about anything that is a part of their daily life.
Physical symptoms:

- Fatigues

- Muscle tension

- Teeth grinding

- Sweating

- Rapid breathing

Psychological symptoms:

- Intense fear of failure

- Memory difficulties

- Excessive overthinking

- Constantly indecisive
Social Anxiety Disorder
The intense fear, or anxiety of dealing with social situations. Often times, people with SAD worry they will be negatively judged because of their actions which is why many tend to avoid going out and interacting with many people.
What counts as a social situation? There is generally two types that can be caterogiezed. Performance situations and interpersonal situations:

Physical Symptoms:

- Racing heart

- Upset stomach

- Shaking

- Nausea

- Shortness of breath

- Sweating

Psychological Symptoms:

- Negative thoughts

- Purposefully ignoring/avoiding situations
Panic Disorder
When an individual experiences reccuring, sudden panic attacks. Panic attacks are described as feeling an overwhelming amount of fear that increases within minutes. Panic disorder is seen as the constant worry/fear of having another panic attack.
Physical Symptoms:

- Dizziness

- Shortness of breath

- Racing heartbeat

- Shaking

- Chest pain or tightness

Psychological Symptoms:

-Feeling of being detached from yourself or your own body

-Inability to grasp reality
